About Dr. Amara Njoki

A psychologist who believes healing is yours to reclaim.

For over a decade, I've been walking alongside individuals through some of life's most difficult terrain — and the most profound transformations.

Nairobi, Kenya & Online

My Story

Psychology isn't just my profession — it's my calling.

I came to psychology through my own experiences of loss and reinvention. Growing up, I watched people around me carry enormous pain in silence — not because they were weak, but because no one had shown them how to carry it differently. That silence lit something in me.

Today, I work with adults navigating anxiety, burnout, grief, relationship challenges, trauma, and the particular kind of exhaustion that comes from performing wellness while quietly unraveling. My clients come from all walks of life, and what they share is a courage that often surprises them when they discover it.

"The curious paradox is that when I accept myself just as I am, then I can change."

— Carl Rogers, Founder of Person-Centered Therapy

I trained in Nairobi and continued postgraduate studies in clinical psychology with a focus on trauma-informed care. My approach draws from Person-Centered Therapy, Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), and Acceptance & Commitment Therapy (ACT) — but above everything else, I follow your lead. You are the expert on your own life.

When I'm not in session, you'll find me writing about the intersection of mental health and everyday African life, which is a conversation I think is long overdue. I believe that psychological wellness is not a luxury — it is infrastructure.

What I Stand For

The principles that guide every session.

01

You are not broken

Struggling does not mean something is fundamentally wrong with you. It means you are human. My work starts from a place of radical acceptance, not pathology.

02

Safety is non-negotiable

A therapy room where you don't feel safe is just a room. Every session is held in confidence, judgment-free, and paced entirely by you.

03

Culture is context

Your identity, your family dynamics, your cultural background are not footnotes to your mental health story — they are often the whole story. I work with them, not around them.

04

Change is possible, always

Decades of neuroscience confirm what I've seen in session after session: the brain retains its capacity to change. Healing is not wishful thinking — it is documented biology.

How I Work

Evidence-based therapy that actually feels human.

I draw from several therapeutic modalities depending on what each individual needs. There's no one-size-fits-all approach to healing — and I'm suspicious of anyone who says otherwise.

Practically, this means our work together might include exploring the thoughts and patterns that keep you stuck, processing difficult memories in a body-aware way, learning to respond rather than react to overwhelming emotions, or simply talking through a life decision with someone genuinely in your corner.

  • Person-Centered Therapy (PCT) — the foundational lens. Your autonomy, your pace, your wisdom.
  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) — identifying and restructuring unhelpful thought patterns.
  • Acceptance & Commitment Therapy (ACT) — building psychological flexibility and value-driven living.
  • Trauma-Informed Care — all sessions are held with an awareness of how trauma shapes the mind and body.
  • Psychoeducation — understanding your mind is part of healing it. I explain as we go.
